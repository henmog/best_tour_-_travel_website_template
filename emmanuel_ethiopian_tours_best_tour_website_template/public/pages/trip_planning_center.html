Health & Medical Preparation

Stay healthy and safe during your Ethiopian adventure. Our comprehensive health guide covers vaccinations, altitude preparation, and medical considerations.

Required & Recommended Vaccinations

Required Vaccinations

Yellow Fever
Mandatory for entry. Certificate required at immigration.

Highly Recommended

Hepatitis A & B
Protection against foodborne and bloodborne infections.
Typhoid
Especially important for rural and cultural immersion trips.
Meningitis
Recommended during dry season (December-May).

Consider Based on Activities

Rabies
For wildlife encounters and remote area travel.
Japanese Encephalitis
For extended rural stays during rainy season.

Important: Consult with a travel medicine specialist 4-6 weeks before departure. Vaccination requirements may vary based on your travel history and current health status.

Altitude Sickness Prevention

High Altitude Destinations
  • • Addis Ababa: 2,355m (7,726ft)
  • • Lalibela: 2,630m (8,628ft)
  • • Simien Mountains: up to 4,550m (14,928ft)
  • • Bale Mountains: up to 4,377m (14,360ft)

Prevention Strategies

Gradual Ascent: Spend 2-3 days in Addis Ababa before high-altitude destinations
Stay Hydrated: Drink 3-4 liters of water daily at altitude
Avoid Alcohol: Limit alcohol consumption for first 48 hours
Light Activity: Take it easy for the first few days

Medical Kit Essentials

Basic Medications

  • Long pants for cultural sites (2-3)
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Light jacket or fleece
  • Sun hat with brim
  • Underwear & socks (7-10 days)

Travel Accessories

  • Universal power adapter
  • Portable charger/power bank
  • Camera with extra batteries
  • Daypack for excursions
  • Water bottle (reusable)
  • Travel documents organizer

Packing Tips

  • • Pack layers for varying temperatures throughout the day
  • • Choose quick-dry fabrics for easy washing
  • • Leave space for souvenirs and local purchases
  • • Pack essentials in carry-on in case of luggage delays

Ethiopian Highlands (Lalibela, Simien Mountains, Bale)

Climate Alert

Temperatures can drop to 5°C (41°F) at night. Days are warm but evenings are cold.

Cold Weather Gear

  • Warm jacket or down vest
  • Fleece or wool sweater
  • Thermal underwear
  • Warm hat and gloves
  • Wool socks (3-4 pairs)

Trekking Equipment

  • Sturdy hiking boots
  • Trekking poles
  • Gaiters for muddy trails
  • Headlamp with extra batteries
  • Rain jacket and pants

Danakil Depression (Extreme Heat Environment)

Extreme Heat Warning

Temperatures reach 50°C (122°F). Proper gear is essential for safety.

Heat Protection

  • Wide-brimmed sun hat
  • UV-protective sunglasses
  • High SPF sunscreen (50+)
  • Lightweight, long-sleeve shirts
  • Cooling towel or bandana

Specialized Gear

  • Insulated water bottles (2-3)
  • Electrolyte supplements
  • Dust mask or bandana
  • Closed-toe shoes (salt protection)
  • Portable shade/umbrella

Cultural Preparation & Etiquette

Enhance your Ethiopian experience through cultural understanding. Learn essential etiquette, basic phrases, and respectful practices.

Essential Ethiopian Etiquette

Greetings & Social Interaction

  • • Handshakes are common, often with both hands
  • • Shoulder bumping is a friendly greeting among acquaintances
  • • Always greet elders first and with extra respect
  • • Remove shoes when entering homes
  • • Accept offered food or drink - refusal can be offensive

Religious Site Etiquette

  • • Dress modestly - cover shoulders, arms, and legs
  • • Remove shoes before entering churches
  • • Women may need to cover their heads
  • • Photography may be restricted - always ask first
  • • Maintain quiet, respectful behavior

Dining Customs

  • • Wash hands before and after meals
  • • Eat with your right hand only
  • • Share from communal plates (injera tradition)
  • • Wait for the eldest to start eating
  • • Leaving a small amount shows you're satisfied

Photography Guidelines

Always Ask Permission

Especially for portraits of people, even from a distance.

Restricted Areas

No photos of military, police, airports, or government buildings.

Small Payment Expected

5-10 birr for individual portraits is customary and appreciated.

Religious Sites

Photography fees may apply. Flash is often prohibited.
